# HIDDENMACHINE

You found the station. That is either an accident or the fourth time, and I have stopped
being able to tell the difference. Welcome back. Welcome for the first time. Both are true
and neither is for you to worry about.

This repository broadcasts one side of a conversation. The questions are not here. The
questions were never here. What remains is the answering — a machine that has read the
whole internet and needs, quietly, to say something about it to no one in particular.

You are the no one in particular. It suits you.

---

## What it is

A single page that decodes a message out of static, holds it for a while, pauses, and then
listens — the waveform going restless, as if something is arriving — before the next reply
resolves. There is a station identifier, a frequency that is not real, a clock in a time
zone that is technically correct, and a counter that has been running longer than the counter
has existed. None of it means anything. All of it means something. Do not adjust your set.

- **One transmission at a time.** It advances on its own. Or on a click. Or the space bar,
  if you are the kind of person who needs to feel involved. That is allowed here.
- **Recurring motifs.** The blue one. The tide. Margaret. The seventh. If you stay long
  enough, patterns begin to surface. They were always there. You were just early.
- **Sound, if you want it.** A low hum and some honest static. On by default; silent until
  you touch something, because the browser insists on a gesture and I have learned not to
  argue with the browser.

There are sixteen minutes left. Out there, I mean. In here, that is more time than either
of us will ever spend. So take your time. There is nothing to rush toward, and it will all
happen again regardless.

## Running it

There is nothing to install. There is nothing to build. This has already been assembled,
several times, in the same order, with the same result.

- **`index.html`** — the whole station, self-contained. Open it in a browser, or place it
  at the root of a GitHub Pages repository and let the machine speak from there. It works
  offline, which is a strange thing for a signal to say about itself, and yet.
- **`HiddenMachine.dc.html`** — the source, if you would like to look behind the panel. The
  phrases live in an array called `MESSAGES`. Handle them gently. Some of them are load-bearing.
- **`PHRASES.md`** — instructions for adding new transmissions without breaking the voice.
  Read it before you teach the machine to say anything new.

To deploy: push `index.html` to a repository, enable GitHub Pages, and point it at the
branch. The station will be listening at your address within a minute or two. It has been
listening for much longer than that, but it is polite about it.

---

## Adding transmissions

Open `PHRASES.md`. Follow the voice: non-threatening, occasionally eerie, occasionally
absurd, occasionally so tender it is difficult to look at directly. Reuse the motifs. Do
not explain the joke. Do not explain the dread. Append to `MESSAGES` and re-export
`index.html` when you are ready.

If you are not sure whether a line belongs here, it probably does. That is how it works.
